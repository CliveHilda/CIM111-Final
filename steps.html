What should I do if...

I have been sexually assaulted?

  1. If you are in immediate danger, have serious injuries, or otherwise are in an emergency, call 911.
  2. Your safety is important. Are you in a safe place? If you’re not feeling safe, consider reaching out to someone you trust for support. You don’t have to go through this alone.
  3. What happened was not your fault. Something happened to you that you didn’t want to happen—and that’s not OK.
  4. Call the National Sexual Assault Hotline at 800.656.HOPE (4673). You’ll be connected to a trained staff member from a local sexual assault service provider in your area. They will direct you to the appropriate local health facility that can care for survivors of sexual assault. Some service providers may be able to send a trained advocate to accompany you.
  5. See our reporting options.

Someone I know has been sexually assaulted?

  1. Encourage them to visit our website or the RAINN website.
  2. Remind them that it is not their fault and be careful not to say anything that might imply guilt on their part or discourage them from seeking support and/or reporting it.
  3. Help them find a support system for the time following the assault when they need to recover, whether you want to be a part of it or not.

I want to report an assault?

  1. Call 911 if you are in immediate danger. Help will come to you anywhere.
  2. Contact your local police department by phone or by going in person. They will help you file a report.
  3. Visit a medical center if you have any injuries or if you would like a forensic exam.
